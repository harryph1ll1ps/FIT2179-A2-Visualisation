# Death rates and emissions by energy source

- **File:** `death-rates-by-energy-source.csv`
- **Powers:** log–log bubble chart — *"Safe and Clean — or Neither?"*
- **Source:** [Our World in Data — Nuclear Energy](https://ourworldindata.org/nuclear-energy), drawing on Markandya & Wilkinson (2007), Sovacool et al. (2016) and the IPCC AR5 life-cycle emissions assessment.
- **Coverage:** 8 energy sources, reference year 2021.

## Columns
| Column | Description |
| --- | --- |
| `Entity` | Energy source (Coal, Oil, Natural Gas, Biomass, Hydropower, Wind, Nuclear, Solar). |
| `deaths` | Deaths per terawatt-hour (TWh) of electricity produced (accidents + air pollution). |
| `ghg` | Life-cycle greenhouse-gas emissions, tonnes CO₂-equivalent per gigawatt-hour (GWh). |
| `share` | Source's share of global electricity generation (%). |
| `Category` | Grouping used for colour: `Fossil fuel`, `Renewable` or `Nuclear`. |
| `Year` | Reference year (2021). |
